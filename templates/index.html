How it works

In 1935, Erwin Schrödinger described a paradox: a cat in a sealed box is simultaneously alive and dead until observed. A radioactive atom inside is in quantum superposition — decayed and undecayed at once — and only collapses to one state when measured.

When you click the button, we run a real quantum circuit on an IBM Quantum processor. A Hadamard gate puts a qubit into perfect superposition: |ψ⟩ = 1/√2 (|0⟩ + |1⟩). Measuring it collapses the wave function. |0⟩ = alive, |1⟩ = dead — exactly 50/50, guaranteed by physics.
